Taylor Swift

Taylor Swift on her record-breaking tour, Taylor Swift: The Eras Tour.

Here's a timeline of key events in Taylor Swift's life.

  • Taylor Alison Swift is born in Reading, Pennsylvania.
  • Swift's family relocates to Wyomissing, Pennsylvania. Taylor becomes interested in music and starts performing at local events.
  • Swift begins visiting Nashville, Tennessee, to pursue a career in country music.
  • She signs a music publishing deal with Sony/ATV, becoming the youngest signing in the company's history.
  • Taylor Swift releases her self-titled debut album, which includes hit singles like "Tim McGraw" and "Teardrops on My Guitar."
  • Swift releases her second album, "Fearless," which includes hits like "Love Story" and "You Belong with Me."
  • She wins four Grammy Awards, including Album of the Year, making her the youngest person to receive this honor.
  • Swift releases the album "Speak Now," entirely written by herself.
  • "Red," her fourth studio album, is released, featuring a mix of country and pop elements.
  • Swift releases "1989," a full-pop album, which includes chart-toppers like "Shake It Off" and "Blank Space."
  • Swift takes a hiatus from public appearances and social media.
  • She returns with the album "Reputation," showcasing a darker and edgier image.
  • Swift releases "Lover," a more romantic and upbeat album.
  • During the COVID-19 pandemic, Swift surprises fans by releasing two albums, "Folklore" and "Evermore," showcasing a shift to indie and alternative sounds.
  • Swift begins re-recording her early albums after a dispute over the ownership of her master recordings.
  • She releases "Fearless (Taylor's Version)" and later "Red (Taylor's Version)."
  • Swift is involved in activism, speaking out on issues such as LGBTQ+ rights and the 2020 United States presidential election.
  • In November, she releases "Red (Taylor's Version)," which includes new recordings and additional songs.
